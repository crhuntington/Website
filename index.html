Our story

We built Hearthward because we needed it.

Over ten years ago, we bought our first home. We were excited, grateful, and completely clueless about what it actually meant to own it. Our agent was wonderful — patient, knowledgeable, a trusted friend. Then closing day came. And that was that.

We learned about home maintenance the way most homeowners do: through costly mistakes. A water heater that hadn't been flushed in years. A furnace filter untouched since move-in. A roof that probably could have held on longer with one annual inspection.

The information existed. The expertise existed — in the head of our agent, the people we trusted most with our biggest purchase. What didn't exist was a way to stay connected, to keep that expertise accessible, to make homeownership feel managed rather than reactive. That's why Hearthward exists.

Our mission

To make homeownership feel like something you've got handled.

We believe every homeowner deserves a knowledgeable friend in their corner — someone who knows their home, their mortgage, their market. Hearthward makes that permanent.

The problem we solve

The most important relationship in real estate ends at the wrong moment.

Homeowners trust their agent more than almost any professional in their lives — more than their doctor for the biggest financial decision they'll ever make. Then closing day arrives, the champagne is poured, and that relationship quietly ends.

The result: only 26% of homeowners return to their original agent for their next transaction. Not because they were unhappy — because they simply lost touch. Life moved on. The relationship faded.

For agents, that's $47,000 in average commission lost per missed repeat transaction. For lenders, it's a refinance going to a competitor the moment rates shift. For homeowners, it's navigating their largest investment without guidance.

Hearthward solves all three problems at once.
